  • ISSUE I - DAREECHA (WINDOW)

    What is a window? The simplest answer would be to describe a wooden structure fitted with glass that allows you to gaze through it. However, in true literary fashion, we must question what it represents as a symbol, as a metaphor. Windows have always been a source of attraction for artists and writers alike, a trigger to curiosity, to wonder, to invaded intimacy. There is something inherently invasive about gazing at the world from behind a structure, a disconnect and yet a brittle connection. What do you see when you imagine a window within your mind? What do you feel when you look through and find someone looking right back? Is there loneliness when you see an act of love shrouded in scarlet lighting? Is there pleasure in witnessing a lone human sitting by themselves? What is a window to you?
